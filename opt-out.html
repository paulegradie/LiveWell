What happens if you don’t join the trust?


The plan fails without full participation


Why?

The strategy depends on everyone pooling resources so that the trust can pay off debt quickly and convert cash flow into high‑yield rentals. If one or more family members opt out, the remaining group carries the same or higher debt with less income. That breaks the engine that makes the plan work.

  • Debt burden stays too high: We continue servicing large repayments that soak up monthly cash.
  • Rental income can’t cover obligations: Yields from the first properties are insufficient when debt isn’t shared across the full family base.
  • Paul’s salary becomes the plug: Instead of funding growth and family experiences, salary is diverted to interest and shortfalls.
  • Freedom is delayed: With debt service eating cash, allowances stay low and trips/upgrades get pushed out.

Concrete impacts

  • Fewer properties acquired in the first 2–3 years → slower income growth.
  • Lower monthly allowances for everyone who remains.
  • Reduced surplus for travel, emergencies and helping family overseas.
  • Higher stress and risk for the remaining participants.

Why full buy‑in matters

Pooling resources is how we turn fixed costs into scalable income. With full buy‑in, we eliminate personal debt quickly, stabilise housing costs and channel surplus into assets that raise everyone’s allowance. Without full buy‑in, the trust drags instead of accelerates.

Next steps

  • Review each journey (Mahnaz, Iraj, Paul & Ana) to see the upside with full participation.
  • Discuss questions or concerns early so we can design fair allowances and safeguards.
  • Commit together — the plan only works if we all pull in the same direction.
