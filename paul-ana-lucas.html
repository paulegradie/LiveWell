Ana, Paul & Lucas’s Journey

1. Options: These are your choices

Option 1 · High‑Mortgage Life

Keep the mortgage, separate finances

  • ~$130k/year goes to interest + repayments instead of building wealth
  • Travel and experiences shrink; less time and energy for Lucas
  • Vulnerable to rate hikes, job changes and surprise bills
  • Supers grow slowly; cash locked in the house

Comfortable, but stuck servicing debt.

Option 2 · Family Trust

Stable home, growing income

  • Pay household costs from Trust; live on a modest allowance that grows
  • Sell house → eliminate mortgage → buy income‑producing properties
  • Trips and bigger purchases funded by surplus (not credit cards)
  • Supers + Trust income compound together

More freedom now, more wealth later.

2. Timeline & Experience: Exact steps to join the Trust

Months 0–3

Sell · Clear · Buffer

  • House sells; mortgage cleared; buffer set aside
  • Move to a comfortable rental arranged by the Trust
  • Begin living on an agreed allowance
You get: immediate relief and a clear plan
Month 4

Stabilise · Enjoy

  • Trust pays rent, utilities, insurance and tax
  • Allowance covers day-to-day; no surprise bills
  • Start planning a family trip with surplus
You get: calm months and time with Lucas
Years 1–3

Invest · Income grows

  • Trust buys high‑yield properties
  • Allowances step up as rents flow
  • Trip budgets and extras funded by surplus
You get: more freedom each year
Years 4–10

Comfort · Dignity · Choice

  • Regular overseas trips and family visits
  • Buy a dream home outright if desired
  • Ongoing support for Lucas’s education
You get: security and options

3. What you get: Why this feels good

Less stress

No more juggling bills. The Trust pays rent, utilities, insurance and tax.

Money you control

A monthly allowance you can use freely — with surplus funding bigger purchases.

Freedom to enjoy

Travel with Lucas and visit family overseas — without touching your capital.

The Trust pays — not you alone.

You’re supporting your lifestyle through property income, not overtime or credit cards. Your participation also makes life easier for parents and builds Lucas’s future.
