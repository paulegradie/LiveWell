How the Family Trust Works

A simple, safe way to pool resources so everyone lives better now and later.

1. What it is 2. How it works 3. Why it’s good 4. Projections

1. What it is: The Family Trust

A trust is a legal entity that holds assets for the benefit of beneficiaries. Its essentially a glorified bank account (but a bit more complex).

Our family trust is a corporate structure established to hold and manage all of our shared assets. It’s designed for both protection and growth. The trust is held 90% in Ana’s name and 10% in Paul’s name to maximise tax efficiency while keeping control within the family. To ensure that pensions remain unaffected, both Mahnaz and Iraj disassociate themselves from legal ownership of the trust’s assets, instead receiving regular allowances as beneficiaries.

We employ professional advisers and accountants to handle all the legal and administrative work. Companies like Ramsay and TriForce ensure that the trust complies with Australian law, files tax returns correctly and maintains proper records. This means beneficiaries never need to worry about paperwork or compliance.

2. How it works: Flow of money and decisions

  • Income flows in: All rental income, pensions and salaries are deposited directly into the trust’s bank account.
  • Expenses are paid: The trust pays for property rates, insurance, maintenance and utilities as well as rent for beneficiaries who don’t own a home.
  • Allowances are distributed: Each beneficiary receives a monthly allowance agreed by the family (e.g. Mahnaz starts at $2,500/month, Iraj at $1,800/month, Paul & Ana around $2,500–$3,000). These allowances are for personal living costs; housing and taxes are handled by the trust.
  • Surplus is reinvested: Any money left over after allowances and expenses is used to purchase new high‑yield rental properties. This reinvestment causes the trust’s income to grow steadily over time.
  • Professionally managed: Accountants prepare the trust’s financial statements and tax returns. Trustees (Ana and Paul) approve major decisions like buying or selling properties.
  • Transparent and inclusive: Beneficiaries may access statements and choose how involved they want to be in investment decisions. Extra funds can be requested for emergencies or special occasions without disrupting the overall plan.

3. Why it’s good: Safe, simple, beneficial

  • Asset protection: A corporate trust separates personal liabilities from trust assets. Creditors can’t claim trust property if an individual runs into trouble.
  • Tax efficiency: Allocating 90% ownership to Ana and 10% to Paul allows distributions to be channelled to the lower tax bracket. This means more of the income stays in the family.
  • Professional oversight: Experienced accountants and advisers ensure compliance with the law and manage complex tasks like tax filings and property settlements.
  • Growing income: Because the trust reinvests surplus cash, the rental income grows year after year. This allows the allowances to increase instead of shrink over time.
  • Flexibility and control: Beneficiaries retain the right to draw extra funds for emergencies or family support without worrying about interest rates or bank approvals.

For someone like Mahnaz, this structure removes the burden of managing a property and worrying about bills. She simply receives her monthly allowance, which grows over time, while knowing there is always a substantial safety net available. It’s a way to enjoy a secure and comfortable life while helping the whole family prosper.
