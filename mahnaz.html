1. Options: These are your retirement choices

Option 1 · Independent

Own apartment, fixed income

  • ~$840k from house sale to purchase apartment
  • Pension + $250k super; income stays flat
  • About $3,241 per month (~$38,900/year) to live on; no growth
  • No trust participation or compounding; purchasing power declines with inflation
  • You handle rates, body corp, insurance, repairs
  • Surprise bills (medical, repairs, family help) are yours alone and eat into super
  • You will depend on Jameleh and Amir for large expenses (like a Europe trip, which Ana could not afford)
  • Savings diminish over time; inflation reduces purchasing power
  • Sets Paul & Ana back by decades; slows allowances for everyone

You will be comfortable with a minimal lifestyle, but it comes at the expense of others. You get peace of mind, but life gets harder for everyone else.

Option 2 · Family Trust

Stable home, growing income

  • Keep $50k personal; rest joins the trust
  • Trust pays rent, utilities, insurance and tax
  • Allowance starts at $2,500/mo → rises toward $3,500
  • Trust pays for emergencies and international trips—without touching your savings
  • You can afford your own large expenses (overseas trips, emergencies) without asking others
  • Everyone has more to spend on themselves because you aren’t a burden
  • All funded by growing property income — not by Paul working more

Less admin, more peace. Income grows over time while capital is protected — and it comes from property income, not from family or your super

Very, very comfortable for you — more than Option 1 — and you make life easier for Paul and Ana. A gift that’s invaluable

2. Timeline & Experience: Exact steps to join the Trust

Months 0–3

  • Contribute $200k of super to the Trust’s offset accounts; keep $50k in your own account
  • We keep this in offset (not into a property) to stay liquid for you and eliminate interest on interest-only loans
  • Stay with Jameleh while the Trust sets up the apartment
  • Trust clears debts and builds a safety buffer
You get: zero admin, clear plan, money in your account
Month 4

  • Trust pays rent, utilities and insurance

  • Keep $50k personal for comfort and gifts
You get: stable home, money you control, no bills
Years 1–3

  • Trust buys 2–3 rental properties
  • Allowance steps up as cash flow grows
  • Emergency funds and trip budgets available
You get: rising allowance; bigger trips and gifts
Years 4–10

Comfort · Dignity · Freedom

  • Business-class travel and better devices
  • Support for family overseas, without stress
  • Capital preserved; income keeps rising
You get: security now and a happier life
