Mahnaz’s Journey

Option 1: Independent Retirement

If Mahnaz decides to sell the family home, buy an apartment and live off her superannuation and pension, this is what her life could look like:

  • She would receive roughly $840k from her share of the house sale.
  • She’d need to purchase an apartment outright and cover ongoing costs like council rates, body corporate fees, maintenance and insurance.
  • Her income would come from her $250k superannuation balance and her government pension (~$550/week).
  • With costs rising and income fixed, her savings would slowly diminish over time.
  • She would be responsible for paying all bills, organising maintenance and managing her own tax paperwork.

This path offers independence, but her income never grows. Any unexpected expense — a medical bill or helping family overseas — comes directly from her shrinking nest egg.

Option 2: Joining the Family Trust

By pooling her proceeds into the family trust, Mahnaz unlocks a growing income and complete peace of mind:

  • She contributes her sale proceeds into the trust and keeps $50k in her personal account for comfort.
  • She stays with Jameleh for a few months while the initial buffer is built, then moves into a comfortable apartment with rent and utilities paid by the trust.
  • She receives a monthly allowance that starts at $2,500 and gradually rises to $3,500 per month as the trust grows.
  • Her allowance is purely for personal living expenses – the trust handles rent, rates, insurance, tax filings and major repairs.
  • As a beneficiary, she always has visibility into trust accounts and can request extra funds for emergencies or special trips.
  • The trust’s investments generate increasing cash flow each year, enabling bigger family travel budgets, business‑class flights, new phones or tablets and ongoing support for Lucas’s education.

This path protects Mahnaz’s savings, provides ever‑increasing income and eliminates the stress of managing property. She remains in control — deciding how involved she wants to be — while enjoying a simpler, more abundant life.

Timeline & Experience

After the house sells, the trust pays off all debts and builds a cash buffer. Over the next few years it purchases high‑yield rental properties that produce steady income:

  • Months 0–3: Sale settles; Mahnaz stays with Jameleh while we secure a rental apartment and build the trust buffer.
  • Month 4: She moves into her new apartment and begins receiving her monthly allowance.
  • Years 1–3: The trust buys two to three investment properties; her allowance increases gradually.
  • Years 4–10: The portfolio snowballs, enabling upgrades like business‑class travel, better devices and regular gifts to family abroad while the principal continues to grow.

Throughout this journey Mahnaz never worries about surprise bills or downturns — the trust’s liquidity buffer and professional advisers ensure stability. Her legacy helps build a better future for Lucas while securing her own comfort.
