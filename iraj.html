Iraj’s Journey

Two simple paths for Iraj. We’ll compare outcomes, show the steps, and highlight what he gets.

1. Options: These are your choices

Option 1 · Independent

Rent apartment, fixed income

  • ~$800k from selling your house + ~$550/week pension
  • Pay ~ $2,000/month in rent for up to 3 years while the QLD apartment is built (if it finishes on time)
  • Pension must stretch to cover groceries, utilities, phone and healthcare during that time
  • If you buy the QLD apartment outright, most of the $800k is gone; you may have ~ $150k savings + pension to live on
  • No growth mechanism; rising costs reduce purchasing power
  • You handle all bills, repairs and admin; surprise bills eat into savings

Comfortable, but tight — and you carry everything alone.

Option 2 · Family Trust

Stable home, growing income

  • Trust pays rent, utilities, insurance and tax
  • Allowance starts at $1,800/mo and rises toward $2,500 as the trust grows
  • Emergency funds and international trips covered — without touching savings
  • All funded by growing property income — not by family sacrifice

More comfort for you, less stress for everyone.

2. Steps: Exact steps to join the Trust

Months 0–3

Join · Stabilise

  • Move into a Trust‑funded apartment; settle utilities
  • Trust covers rent, insurance and essential bills
  • Build a small buffer for comfort and upcoming needs
You get: stability, no admin, and breathing room
Month 4

Allowance starts

  • Receive your monthly allowance ($1,800+)
  • Trust handles repairs and admin
  • Emergency funds available when needed
You get: money you control and no surprise bills
Years 1–3

Invest · Income grows

  • Trust buys high‑yield properties
  • Allowance steps up toward $2,500
  • Trip budgets and emergency funds covered
You get: rising allowance and more comfort
Years 4–10

Comfort · Dignity · Freedom

  • Regular travel and better devices
  • Support family without stress
  • Capital preserved; income keeps growing
You get: security and ease

3. What you get: Why this feels good

Less stress

No more bills or property admin. The Trust pays rent, utilities, insurance and tax.

Money you control

Allowance each month you can use freely for groceries, phone, gifts and outings.

Freedom to enjoy

Travel and help family without dipping into savings.

The Trust pays — not your family.

You are not being supported by others; you’re supporting yourself through property income. Your participation actually makes life easier for everyone.

4. How this helps the family

  • More stability at home means fewer crises for everyone
  • Property income grows allowances without extra work
  • Trips and emergencies come from the Trust, not from Paul

Your choice removes pressure from others and builds a better base for the whole family.

Timeline & Experience

The trust grows steadily after inception. Iraj’s experience follows a similar arc to Mahnaz’s:

  • Months 0–3: Trust formation and initial buffer build; Iraj continues in his current accommodation.
  • Month 4: He moves into a trust‑funded apartment and begins receiving his monthly allowance.
  • Years 1–3: The trust acquires several high‑yield properties; his allowance edges up toward $2,500.
  • Years 4–10: Income growth accelerates, enabling generous travel, gifts and support for extended family without eroding capital.

This path offers Iraj the dignity of choice and the security of shared resources. By working together, the family ensures everyone benefits from rising tides.
