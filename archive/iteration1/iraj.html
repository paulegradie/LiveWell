Iraj’s Journey

Option 1: Independent Living

If Iraj continues to live independently on his pension, he would:

  • Pay market rent of about $800/week (over $41k/year) for his own apartment.
  • Cover all bills — utilities, phone, insurance, healthcare and groceries — from his modest pension and small savings.
  • Watch his budget remain tight and fixed; there is no mechanism for his income to grow over time.
  • Handle all housing issues, maintenance and administrative work himself.

This provides maximum independence but leaves little buffer for travel, supporting family or weathering emergencies. Any change in costs directly reduces his quality of life.

Option 2: Joining the Family Trust

Pooling his resources with the family trust gives Iraj stability and upside:

  • He adds his savings and pension income to the trust; the trust covers his rent, utilities and insurance from day one.
  • He receives a monthly allowance starting at $1,800 and growing to $2,500 as the trust grows.
  • All housing, tax filings and maintenance are handled by the trust, freeing him from bureaucratic headaches.
  • Emergency funds and travel budgets are available without dipping into his personal savings.
  • As the portfolio expands, his allowance increases annually and he benefits from the same luxuries: family trips, new devices and peace of mind.

By joining the trust, Iraj trades isolation and financial pressure for stability, growth and support. He remains in control of his lifestyle choices while sharing in a growing pool of resources.

Timeline & Experience

The trust grows steadily after inception. Iraj’s experience follows a similar arc to Mahnaz’s:

  • Months 0–3: Trust formation and initial buffer build; Iraj continues in his current accommodation.
  • Month 4: He moves into a trust‑funded apartment and begins receiving his monthly allowance.
  • Years 1–3: The trust acquires several high‑yield properties; his allowance edges up toward $2,500.
  • Years 4–10: Income growth accelerates, enabling generous travel, gifts and support for extended family without eroding capital.

This path offers Iraj the dignity of choice and the security of shared resources. By working together, the family ensures everyone benefits from rising tides.
